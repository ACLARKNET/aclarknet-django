    Our involvement in open source software development

    In 2004, inspired by the Plone CMS community, Alex Clark began contributing to open source software development.

    For the next several years, he made significant contributions to several Plone add-ons including Products.PloneHelpCenter, Products.PloneSoftwareCenter, and Products.PloneOrg.

    In 2009, he completed his first Plone Improvment Proposal: Add ability to choose group when adding new site members.

    In 2010, he forked the Python Imaging Library into the Pillow project. As of early 2015, Pillow has been downloaded over 5 million times and has replaced PIL in many major Linux distributions, including Ubuntu & Fedora Core.

    In 2011, he completed his second Plone Improvement Proposal: Add 'test mail server' form to Mail Settings.

    In 2013, in an effort to simplify the Plone installation process, he began the Plock project.

    This website, launched in early 2015, is open source.
